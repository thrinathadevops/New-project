# First Candle Rule - Complete Trading Guide

## 🕐 What is the First Candle Rule?

The **First Candle Rule** is a powerful opening-range breakout technique that appears **every single day** at **9:30-9:35 AM** on the 5-minute chart. This single candle contains more trading information than all other candles combined.

### The Big Picture

```
Each day's battle between buyers and sellers tells a story
in the opening 5-minute candle (9:30-9:35 AM).

This candle defines:
✅ The key price levels for the entire day
✅ The initial market bias (bullish or bearish)
✅ Where consistent winning trades will come from
✅ The exact entry, stop, and target levels
```

## 📊 How It Works (The 5-Step Process)

### Step 1: Wait for Opening Candle (9:30-9:35 AM)

**On your 5-minute chart:**
- Observe the candle that forms from 9:30 to 9:35 AM
- Record its **HIGH** and **LOW**
- These become your KEY LEVELS for the entire day

**Example:**
```
Opening Candle:
- High: ₹2,100.00 ← KEY LEVEL (buyers fought here)
- Low:  ₹2,080.00 ← KEY LEVEL (sellers fought here)
- Close: ₹2,095.00 (buyers won slightly)
```

**What it tells you:**
- If close > open: **Bullish opening** (buyers in control)
- If close < open: **Bearish opening** (sellers in control)
- If close ≈ open: **Neutral opening** (indecision)

### Step 2: Wait for Price to Break These Levels

**After the opening candle, watch the market:**

On subsequent 5-minute candles, price will attempt to:
- Break **ABOVE** the opening high, OR
- Break **BELOW** the opening low

But don't enter yet! We need MORE confirmation.

**Example:**
```
9:35-9:40: Price stays between ₹2,080-₹2,100 (consolidation)
9:40-9:45: Price moves UP
9:45-9:50: Price tries to break ₹2,100
```

### Step 3: Look for Fair Value Gap (FVG) Formation

This is KEY! When price breaks the opening level, we need to see a **GAP** - not just a wick touch or candle closure.

**What's a Real Gap?**
- **NOT ENOUGH:** Price touches high/low but wick retraces
- **NOT ENOUGH:** Price closes slightly outside the level
- **✅ YES:** Price creates a gap between candle wicks

**Bullish Example:**
```
Opening high: ₹2,100.00

✗ Price at 9:50: High: ₹2,102, Low: ₹2,098 (overlaps opening high - NO GAP)
✓ Price at 10:00: High: ₹2,105, Low: ₹2,101 (LOW is above high - GAP FORMED!)
```

**Why the gap matters:**
- A gap shows the market moved with **REAL FORCE**
- Wicks not overlapping means price didn't respect the level
- This tells us who won the battle (buyers or sellers)

### Step 4: Wait for Engulfing Candle Confirmation

Once the FVG forms, we DON'T enter immediately. We wait for ONE MORE candle.

**What's an Engulfing Candle?**
- **Bullish Engulfing:** Close > previous high AND Open < previous close
- **Bearish Engulfing:** Close < previous low AND Open > previous close

**Example:**
```
Gap formed (FVG bullish):
- Previous candle: High ₹2,110, Low ₹2,101

Confirmation candle:
- Open: ₹2,102 (opens within previous)
- Close: ₹2,115 (closes ABOVE previous high)
- This is BULLISH ENGULFING ✓
```

**Why we wait:**
- Engulfing confirms the breakout is real
- Filters out false breakouts
- Ensures we're trading with momentum, not against it

### Step 5: Enter with 3:1 Risk:Reward ⭐

Once you see the engulfing candle, you have your trade setup.

**Trading Plan:**
```
Entry: Price of the engulfing candle (or break level immediately)
Stop: Opposite side of the opening candle
Target: Entry + (3 × Risk distance)

Example (BULLISH):
Opening high: ₹2,100
Opening low: ₹2,080

FVG break above ₹2,100 + engulfing candle
Entry: ₹2,105
Stop Loss: ₹2,080 (risk = ₹25)
Target: ₹2,105 + (3 × ₹25) = ₹2,180 (reward = ₹75)
Risk:Reward = 1:3 ⭐
```

  → Momentum breaks above opening high = STRONG BUY

If sellers won the opening candle:

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

**Q: Opening candle breaks but 15 minutes later price comes back - should I exit?**  
A: No! The engulfing candle confirms the breakout. If you entered on engulfing, trust it.

**Q: What if there's a gap at market open (9:15-9:30)?**  
A: Ignore it. Focus only on the 9:30-9:35 candle. That's your opening candle.

**Q: Can I trade this on 1-minute chart?**  
A: Not recommended. 5-minute chart is optimal. 1-minute is too noisy.

**Q: What if no breakout happens all day?**  
A: Some days are low-volatility. Wait for another day. Don't force trades.

**Q: Should I trade multiple breakouts on the same day?**  
A: Yes! If price breaks above opening high in morning and creates new engulfing, that's another trade.

**Q: Can I use this on 15-minute chart?**  
A: You can, but it's less reliable. 5-minute is optimal for First Candle Rule.
